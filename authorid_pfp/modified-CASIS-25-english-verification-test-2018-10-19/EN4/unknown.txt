The Sudan before the referendum was the largest country in Africa with an area of 25,05,813 square kilometers, making it the 10th largest country in the world. Its population is 27 million. Its farthest border spans two cultural regions of Africa: northern Islam and southern Christianity. More than 400 different languages and dialects are used in 597 ethnic groups in the Sudan. The United Nations classifies it as a low-income, food-deficit country, ranking 147th out of 177 in human development. The new country covers an area of 6,19,745 km (twice the rate of Rajasthan or Madhya Pradesh) and has a population of 90 rupees (7 times less than the population of Rajasthan).
